Manali & Kasol, India

Mountain Views • Adventure Sports • Culture

Overview

Manali is a hill station in the Indian state of Himachal Pradesh, nestled in the Himalayas. Known for its breathtaking mountain views, adventure sports like paragliding and skiing, and rich cultural heritage, Manali is a popular destination for tourists seeking both relaxation and excitement.

Best time to visit

The best time to visit Manali is from October to March when the weather is pleasant and ideal for exploring the mountains and engaging in outdoor activities.

Top Attractions
  • Manali Hill Station
  • Rohtang Pass
  • Hadimba Temple
  • Solang Valley
Adventure Sports
  • Paragliding: Experience the thrill of flying over the stunning landscapes of Manali.
  • Skiing: Enjoy skiing in the Solang Valley during the winter months.
  • River Rafting: Navigate the rapids of the Beas River for an adrenaline-pumping experience.
Famous Festivals
  • Dussehra: A vibrant festival celebrated with processions, music, and dance.
  • Lohri: A traditional festival marking the end of winter, celebrated with bonfires and folk dances.
  • Kullu Dussehra: A unique celebration held in Kullu, featuring traditional music, dance, and rituals.
Cultural Experiences
  • Hadimba Temple: A unique temple dedicated to the goddess Hadimba, surrounded by cedarforests.
  • Manu Temple: A temple dedicated to the sage Manu, located in Old Manali.
  • Local Markets: Explore the vibrant local markets for handicrafts, woolens, and souvenirs.
Travel Tips
  • Carry warm clothing, especially if visiting during the winter months.
  • Be prepared for changing weather conditions in the mountains.
  • Respect the local culture and traditions, especially when visiting religious sites.
  • Try the local cuisine, including Himachali dishes and street food.
