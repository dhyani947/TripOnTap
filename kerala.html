Kerala, India

Backwaters • Beaches • Culture • Adventure

Overview

Kerala is a state in India known for its beautiful backwaters, pristine beaches, rich culture, and lush green landscapes. It is a popular tourist destination that offers a perfect blend of relaxation and adventure.

Best time to visit

The best time to visit Kerala is from September to March when the weather is pleasant and ideal for exploring the backwaters and enjoying outdoor activities.

Top Attractions
  • Backwaters: Alleppey, Kumarakom, Kollam
  • Beaches: Kovalam, Varkala, Cherai
  • Culture: Kathakali, Theyyam, Traditional Arts
  • Festivals: Onam, Thrissur Pooram, Nehru Trophy Boat Race
Adventure Activities
  • Houseboat Cruises: Experience the serene backwaters of Kerala on a traditional houseboat.
  • Trekking: Explore the lush green hills and forests of Wayanad and Munnar.
  • Water Sports: Enjoy activities like kayaking, canoeing, and jet skiing at the beaches.
Cultural Experiences
  • Kathakali: Witness the traditional dance-drama performance that tells stories from Indian epics.
  • Theyyam: Experience the vibrant and colorful ritual dance form of North Kerala.
  • Traditional Arts: Explore the rich artistic heritage of Kerala through its crafts,music, and dance forms.
Famous Festivals
  • Onam: A harvest festival celebrated with grand feasts, traditional games, and cultural performances.
  • Thrissur Pooram: A spectacular temple festival featuring elephant processions, fireworks, and traditional music.
  • Nehru TrophyBoatRace: An excitingboatrace held annually in the Punnamada Lake, attracting teams from across the state.
Temples & Churches
  • Sree Padmanabhaswamy Temple: A famous Hindu templededicated to Lord Vishnu, known for its intricate architecture and rich history.
  • St. Francis Church: The oldest European church in India, located in Kochi, known for itscolonial architecture and historical significance.
  • Guruvayur Temple: A renownedHindu temple dedicated to Lord Krishna, attracting millions of devotees every year.
Travel Tips
  • Transportation: Kerala has a well-connected network of buses, trains, and taxis. Consider hiring a private car for more flexibility in exploring the region.
  • Local Cuisine: Don't miss trying traditional Kerala dishes like appam, puttu, and seafood delicacies.
  • Respect Local Customs: Kerala has a rich cultural heritage, so be mindful of local customs and traditions while visiting temples and attending festivals.
